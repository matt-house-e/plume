# Discovery Framework — Reference Guide

## Purpose

Extract enough business context to make informed strategic decisions. The discovery phase adapts by business maturity — a pre-PMF founder needs different questions than a scaling company with existing data. The goal is to understand the business, its customers, its competitive context, and its constraints before making any strategic recommendations.

## Core Discovery Questions

### Essential Questions (Ask Every Business)

| # | Question | What It Reveals | Follow-Up If Vague |
|---|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | What does your business do, in one sentence? | Core offering clarity | "If a stranger asked at a party, what would you say?" |
| 2 | Who is your best customer today? | ICP signal | "Describe the last customer who was a joy to work with." |
| 3 | Why do customers choose you over alternatives? | Positioning signal | "What do customers say when they refer you?" |
| 4 | What is the single most important action you want someone to take after finding you? | Conversion goal | "If your website could only have one button, what would it say?" |
| 5 | What are the top 3 objections prospects have? | Messaging barriers | "What questions do people ask before buying?" |
| 6 | What marketing have you tried? What worked? | Channel history | "Even if it was informal — referrals, social posts, anything." |
| 7 | What does success look like in 12 months? | Goal clarity | "Revenue number, customer count, or lifestyle goal?" |
| 8 | How much time and money can you invest in marketing? | Resource constraints | "Hours per week? Monthly budget, even if zero?" |

### Pre-PMF Additional Questions

| # | Question | What It Reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 9 | Have you talked to potential customers about this problem? How many? | Validation level |
| 10 | What did they say when you described the solution? | Problem-solution fit |
| 11 | Would they pay for it today? Have any paid? | Willingness to pay |
| 12 | What are they using right now to solve this problem? | Competitive alternatives |
| 13 | How did you come up with this idea? | Founder insight depth |

### Post-PMF / Revenue Stage Additional Questions

| # | Question | What It Reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 9 | What is your current revenue and growth rate? | Business health |
| 10 | What is your average deal size and sales cycle? | Unit economics |
| 11 | Where do most customers come from today? | Channel signal |
| 12 | What is your churn rate? | Product-market fit strength |
| 13 | Do customers refer you? How often? | Organic growth signal |
| 14 | Who is your biggest competitor? What do they do better? | Competitive context |

### Growth / Scaling Stage Additional Questions

| # | Question | What It Reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 9 | What is your current marketing team structure? | Resource baseline |
| 10 | What channels are active? What are the CAC numbers? | Channel efficiency |
| 11 | What marketing tools are you using? | Tech stack |
| 12 | What is your LTV:CAC ratio? | Unit economics viability |
| 13 | What marketing has failed and why? | Lessons learned |
| 14 | What does your sales process look like? | Marketing-sales alignment |
| 15 | Are there internal politics or constraints affecting marketing? | Hidden blockers |

## Extracting Strategy from Inarticulate Founders

Many founders struggle to articulate their positioning and audience. Use these techniques:

### The "Best Customer" Exercise
Instead of asking "who is your target audience?" (which produces vague answers), ask:
1. "Think of your 3 best customers or clients. What do they have in common?"
2. "Why were they great to work with?"
3. "How did they find you?"
4. "What did they say about you to others?"

Pattern-match across answers to extract ICP characteristics.

### The "Anti-Customer" Exercise
1. "Think of a customer or project that was terrible. What went wrong?"
2. "What kind of person or company should NOT hire you?"

This is often easier to answer and reveals ICP boundaries by exclusion.

### The "Competitor" Exercise
1. "When you lose a deal, who do you lose to? Why?"
2. "When you win a deal, why did they choose you?"
3. "Name 3 companies you admire. What do you like about them?"

This reveals positioning opportunities and competitive context.

### The "Bar Test"
If the founder gives corporate-sounding answers, ask: "Pretend you're at a bar. A friend asks what you do. What do you say?" The informal answer is usually closer to the real positioning than the polished version.

## Discovery Summary Template

After discovery, compile a structured summary:

```markdown
# Discovery Summary

## Business Context
- **Business:** [What they do]
- **Offering:** [Product/service, delivery model, pricing]
- **Revenue:** [Current revenue, growth rate]
- **Stage:** [Pre-PMF / Post-PMF / Growth / Scaling]

## Current Customers
- **Best customers:** [Common characteristics]
- **How they find you:** [Primary acquisition channels]
- **Why they buy:** [Key motivations]
- **Churn/retention:** [Signals of PMF strength]

## Competitive Context
- **Direct competitors:** [Who they lose deals to]
- **Indirect alternatives:** [What customers use instead]
- **Perceived advantage:** [Why customers choose them]

## Marketing Current State
- **What has been tried:** [Channels, tactics, results]
- **What is working:** [Even informally]
- **What has failed:** [And hypotheses about why]
- **Tools in use:** [Current tech stack]

## Resources & Constraints
- **Time available:** [Hours/week for marketing]
- **Budget available:** [Monthly marketing spend]
- **Team:** [Who can execute marketing activities]
- **Constraints:** [Timeline pressures, technical limits, regulatory]

## Goals
- **12-month target:** [Revenue, customers, growth]
- **Primary conversion action:** [What a visitor should do]
- **Success metric:** [How they'll know marketing is working]

## Initial Hypotheses
- **Positioning hypothesis:** [Where they might fit in the landscape]
- **ICP hypothesis:** [Who is most likely to buy]
- **Channel hypothesis:** [Where to reach the ICP]
```

## Input Checklist

Gather as many of these as available before starting strategic work:

- [ ] Business description and offering details
- [ ] Current customer list or descriptions
- [ ] Revenue numbers (even rough)
- [ ] Competitor names and URLs
- [ ] Any existing marketing materials (website, social profiles, content)
- [ ] Analytics access or data (traffic, conversion rates)
- [ ] Previous marketing efforts and results
- [ ] Brand guidelines (if they exist)
- [ ] Budget and time constraints
- [ ] Growth goals and timeline

## Handling Conflicting Existing Strategy

If the user has existing strategy work (from an agency, previous marketer, or their own efforts) that conflicts with what discovery reveals:

1. **Do not discard it silently.** Surface the conflict: "Your agency positioning says X, but your best customers describe you as Y. Which feels more accurate?"
2. **Use it as input, not gospel.** Prior work is a hypothesis to validate, not a constraint to accept.
3. **Test against evidence.** If discovery interviews, competitive data, or conversion data contradicts existing positioning, the data wins.
4. **Preserve what works.** Existing strategy may have elements that are correct. Extract and keep those.
