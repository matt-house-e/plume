# Channel Strategy — Reference Guide

## Purpose

Channel strategy determines where to invest time and money to reach the ICP. The most common startup marketing mistake is spreading thin across too many channels. This reference covers selection frameworks, channel-audience fit, organic and paid channel details, sequencing, and the owned/earned/paid media model.

## Channel Selection Frameworks

### The Bullseye Framework (Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*)

**Step 1 — Outer Ring:** List all channels. Brainstorm at least one idea for each. Do not dismiss any prematurely.

**The 19 Traction Channels:**
1. Targeting blogs / content marketing
2. PR / Publicity
3. Unconventional PR (stunts, contests)
4. SEM / PPC
5. Social and display ads
6. Offline ads
7. SEO
8. Email marketing
9. Viral marketing / referrals
10. Engineering as marketing (free tools)
11. Business development / partnerships
12. Direct sales
13. Affiliate programs
14. Existing platforms (marketplaces, app stores)
15. Trade shows
16. Offline events
17. Speaking engagements
18. Community building
19. Podcasting (modern addition)

**Step 2 — Middle Ring:** Select 3-6 most promising channels based on ICP watering holes, business model, and resources. Run cheap tests on each.

**Step 3 — Inner Ring (Bullseye):** Identify the ONE channel producing the best results. Focus and optimize relentlessly.

**The 50% Rule:** Spend 50% of time on product and 50% on traction. At any stage, have ONE core channel.

### ICE Scoring

Score each candidate channel on three dimensions (1-10 scale):
- **Impact:** How much will this move the needle?
- **Confidence:** How sure are you it will work? (Data = high, gut = low)
- **Ease:** How quickly and cheaply can you test it?

**Score = (Impact + Confidence + Ease) / 3.** Rank channels. Start with the highest-scoring.

### Emily Kramer's MKT1 Channel Status Framework

| Status | Description |
|--------|------------|
| **Core** | Driving 50-80% of growth today |
| **Scaling** | Showing promise, expanding |
| **Testing** | Small-scale experimentation with clear metrics |
| **Up Next** | Fits strategy but not launched |
| **Not Working** | Failed, with documented hypotheses |

Avoid "Random Acts of Marketing" — picking channels from checklists without strategic alignment.

## Channel Sequencing

### The Concentration-First Principle

Master one channel before adding another.

**Recommended sequence:**
1. **Foundation (Month 1):** Website + email list capture
2. **Primary channel (Months 1-3):** ONE visibility channel where ICP spends time. Post 3-5x/week.
3. **Content engine (Months 2-6):** SEO/content as long-term investment. One quality piece/week.
4. **Test paid (Months 3-6):** $1,000-$3,000/month to accelerate proven funnel.
5. **Second channel (Months 6-12):** Only after primary runs on 60-70% of effort.
6. **Earned channels (Month 6+):** Referrals, partnerships, PR — require existing traction.

### When to Add a Second Channel

Primary channel must satisfy four criteria:
1. **Consistent results:** Predictable monthly pipeline
2. **Understood mechanics:** Know exactly why it works
3. **Managed operations:** Runs on 60-70% of effort, not 100%
4. **Identified ceiling:** See diminishing returns approaching

### The 80/20 Rule (Emily Kramer)

80% effort on primary channel, 20% on secondary. Do not split evenly across 5 channels.

## Owned vs Earned vs Paid Media (PESO Framework)

| Type | Definition | Control | Cost | Credibility |
|------|-----------|---------|------|-------------|
| **Paid** | Advertising, sponsorships | Full | Ongoing | Low |
| **Earned** | PR, reviews, word-of-mouth | None | Free (mostly) | Highest |
| **Shared** | Social engagement, community | Partial | Time | Medium |
| **Owned** | Website, blog, email list | Full | Build once | Medium |

### How They Interact

**Paid → Owned → Earned:**
1. Paid drives initial traffic to owned properties
2. Owned content earns trust and creates shareable material
3. Earned media amplifies reach at zero marginal cost

**Why owned is the foundation:** Without a strong website and email list, paid ads have nowhere to send traffic and earned media has nothing to link to. Build owned first, amplify with paid, earn trust over time.

## Content as Distribution Backbone

The 20/80 rule: spend 20% of effort on content creation, 80% on distribution.

One long-form piece should be atomized into:
- 3-5 social media posts
- An email newsletter segment
- A short-form video clip
- A community discussion thread
- A guest post pitch angle
- A paid promotion candidate
