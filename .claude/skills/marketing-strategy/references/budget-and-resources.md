# Budget & Resources — Reference Guide

## Purpose

Every marketing strategy must be executable with the actual resources available. A brilliant strategy the user cannot afford or execute is worthless. This reference covers budget benchmarks, time-as-budget allocation, tool stacks by price tier, and the 70/20/10 allocation framework.

## Marketing Budget Benchmarks

### By Company Stage

| Stage | Marketing as % of Revenue | Annual Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-PMF** | 30-60% (of any revenue) | Time-based primarily |
| **Seed** | 10-20% of funding | $50K-$250K/year |
| **Series A** | 25-40% of funding | $500K-$2M/year |
| **Growth ($1M-$10M)** | 15-25% of revenue | Varies |
| **Mature ($10M+)** | 5-15% of revenue | Varies |

### By Industry (% of Revenue, 2025 Gartner data)

| Industry | Marketing Budget |
|---|---|
| Consumer Products | 9.7% |
| Manufacturing | 9.5% |
| Pharma | 9.0% |
| Tech Services & Products | 7.3% |
| Retail | 7.2% |
| IT and Business Services | 5.8% |
| **Overall Average** | **7.7%** |

### SaaS-Specific

- Median SaaS marketing spend: **8% of ARR**
- High-growth VC-backed: **10-20%+ of ARR**
- Efficient-growth: **<5% of ARR**
- Combined Sales & Marketing: **30-50% of total revenue**
- VC-funded spend 58% more than bootstrapped
- People represent 45-55% of SaaS marketing budgets

### Key Unit Economics

- **Healthy LTV:CAC ratio:** 3:1 or higher
- **LTV:CAC below 2:1:** Business model may not be sustainable
- **CAC payback period target:** Under 12 months
- **Between 2023-2025:** CAC jumped 40-60% across channels

## Time-as-Budget: When Money Is Zero

67% of successful bootstrapped startups spent under $500/month on marketing their first year. The real budget is time.

### 10 Hours/Week Allocation

| Activity | Hours | ROI Potential |
|---|---|---|
| Content creation (1 blog post or long-form piece) | 3-4 | High (compounds) |
| Community engagement (Reddit, forums, social) | 2 | High (40 signups documented from one founder's Reddit participation) |
| Email newsletter | 1-2 | Very High ($36 per $1 spent) |
| Social media (posting + engagement) | 1-2 | Medium-High |
| Analytics & strategy review | 1 | Essential |

### 20 Hours/Week Allocation

Everything above, plus:

| Activity | Hours | ROI Potential |
|---|---|---|
| SEO optimization | 3-4 | Very High (748% ROI over 3 years) |
| Podcast guesting outreach + appearances | 2-3 | Medium (20-100 visitors per appearance) |
| Guest posting | 1-2 | Medium |
| Journalist query platforms (Qwoted, Featured.com) | 1-2 | Medium-High |
| Partnership development | 1-2 | High |

### 40 Hours/Week (Full-Time Marketing)

Everything above, plus:

| Activity | Hours | ROI Potential |
|---|---|---|
| Video content creation | 4-5 | Medium-High |
| Community building (Slack/Discord) | 3-4 | High (5x retention) |
| Micro-influencer partnerships | 2-3 | Medium |
| A/B testing and optimization | 2-3 | High |

### Highest ROI Activities Per Hour (Ranked)

1. **Email marketing** — $36 return per $1 spent
2. **SEO/content** — 748% ROI, 14.6% conversion rate vs 1.7% outbound
3. **Community engagement** — Free, drives referrals and retention
4. **Building in public** — Free, compounds audience over time
5. **Podcast guesting** — Free, targeted audiences
6. **Strategic partnerships** — Free, leverages existing audiences

### The Prioritization Rule

Track hours invested and results per activity. Cut the bottom 50%. Double down on the top 25%.

## The 70/20/10 Budget Framework

Originally popularized by Coca-Cola and Google. McKinsey found companies following balanced allocation achieve 2.7x higher shareholder returns over 10 years.

### The Three Buckets

**70% — Proven Performers:**
- Channels with CAC below 3x average order value
- MER contribution above 3.0
- Predictable volume and clear attribution
- Payback period under 12 months

**20% — Emerging Opportunities:**
- Channels showing early promise, not yet proven at scale
- May have 50% higher initial CAC
- Need 90-day monitoring windows
- Should trend toward benchmarks within 3-6 months

**10% — Experimental:**
- Completely untested ideas
- Expect most to fail
- Minimum 60-day testing period
- Today's experiments become tomorrow's proven channels

### Adaptation by Stage

| Stage | Split | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Startup (<$1M) | 50/30/20 | Need more experiments to find product-channel fit |
| Scale ($1M-$10M) | 70/20/10 | Standard framework |
| Optimization ($10M+) | 80/15/5 | Know what works |

### Unit Economics Override Rule

- CAC x 3 < LTV AND Payback < 12 months → **Scale the channel**
- CAC x 2 < LTV AND Payback < 18 months → **Maintain the channel**
- Otherwise → **Cut or reallocate**

### Rebalancing Schedule

- Monthly: review MER and channel CAC
- Quarterly: deep-dive optimization
- Annually: full strategy overhaul
- Dynamic: adjust when unit economics break
