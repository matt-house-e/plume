# Anti-Patterns — Reference Guide

## Purpose

What NOT to do. Common marketing strategy mistakes organized by category, with quality tests to run at every gate. Recognizing these patterns early prevents wasted time, money, and momentum.

## Strategy-Level Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **No positioning ("we serve everyone")** | Dilutes messaging, increases CAC, makes every downstream decision harder | Use Dunford framework to define specific position. "For [whom], we are the [category] that [differentiator]." |
| **Tactics without strategy** | Creates Random Acts of Marketing (RAM). Activity without direction. | Write the strategy brief first. Define positioning, ICP, messaging, channels, goals BEFORE executing. |
| **Copying competitor tactics** | What works for them depends on their strategy, audience, and resources — not yours. | Study competitors for positioning gaps, not playbook copying. |
| **Strategy by committee** | Compromises produce positioning that appeals to no one. | One person owns the strategy. Get input, then decide. |
| **Annual strategy, never updated** | Markets change faster than annual cycles. | Quarterly tactical reviews. Annual strategic refresh. Dynamic adjustments for market shifts. |
| **Strategy document nobody reads** | A 50-page deck gathering dust is not a strategy. | 5-15 pages max. Clear enough that anyone can articulate it. |

## Positioning Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **"We're the Uber of X"** | Lazy analogy that signals unclear thinking. | Describe what you do and for whom. No analogies needed. |
| **Category creation without resources** | Educating an entire market requires massive sustained investment. | Big Fish, Small Pond — own a subsegment of an existing category. |
| **Positioning by features** | Features are copied; positioning is durable. | Position on value and outcomes, not features. |
| **"Best in class" / "World-class"** | Unverifiable, generic, every competitor says it. | Replace with specific proof: "847 restaurants saved $2.3M in food waste." |
| **Positioning for investors, not customers** | Investor-speak (TAM, disruption) does not resonate with buyers. | Position for the customer. Investors fund businesses customers love. |
| **Refusing to narrow** | "But we might lose customers!" | You will lose the wrong customers. You will attract the right ones. |

## ICP Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **ICP too broad ("small businesses")** | Cannot guide marketing decisions. | Specific enough to disqualify 70% of leads: industry, size, characteristics. |
| **ICP based on wishful thinking** | Describes dream customers, not real ones. | Build ICP from data: who actually buys, retains, and refers? |
| **No anti-ICP** | Without knowing who NOT to pursue, sales wastes time on bad fits. | Define red flags explicitly. |
| **ICP = demographics only** | Demographics do not explain buying behavior. | Add psychographics: values, fears, aspirations, triggers. |
| **Never updated ICP** | As the business grows, the ideal customer evolves. | Revisit quarterly with new customer and churn data. |

## Messaging Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Leading with features** | Customers care about outcomes, not specifications. | Lead with the benefit. Feature is the proof. |
| **"We help businesses grow"** | Any competitor could say it. Fails the "Only We" test. | Be specific: who, what outcome, how, with what proof. |
| **Jargon and buzzwords** | "Leverage synergies to optimize outcomes" means nothing. | Write at grade 6-8 level. Use customer language from interviews. |
| **No proof points** | Claims without evidence are noise. | Every claim needs a number, name, testimonial, or credential. |
| **Same message everywhere** | Awareness levels differ. Homepage visitors ≠ retargeting audience. | Calibrate messaging by Schwartz awareness level. |
| **AI-generated content voice** | In 2025-2026, audiences can detect generic AI copy. Indistinguishable content does not convert. | Inject first-hand experience, genuine opinion, specific stories, imperfect honesty. |

## Channel Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Spreading across 5+ channels** | 20% effort on each means mastering none. | 80% on one channel, 20% on second. Master before adding. |
| **Choosing channels by trend** | "Everyone's on TikTok" is not a strategy. | Choose channels by ICP watering holes. Where do THEY spend time? |
| **Paid before organic proves the funnel** | Paid amplifies what works. If the funnel leaks, paid accelerates the leak. | Prove conversion path organically first, then amplify with paid. |
| **No kill criteria** | Without defined failure criteria, zombie channels drain resources forever. | Pre-define: "If no MQLs after 90 days, we stop." |
| **Equating activity with results** | "We posted 50 times this month" is not a result. | Track downstream metrics: leads, pipeline, revenue per channel. |
| **Channel FOMO** | Adding every new platform out of fear of missing out. | New channels are experiments (10% bucket). Test with rigor, not panic. |

## Metrics Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Vanity metrics dashboard** | Followers, impressions, page views create false confidence. | Track CAC, LTV, conversion rates, revenue per channel. |
| **No attribution** | Cannot determine what is working. | Software attribution + self-reported ("How did you hear about us?"). |
| **Over-attribution** | Crediting every sale to the last click ignores the full journey. | Use multi-touch or blended approaches. |
| **No baseline data** | Setting goals without benchmarks is guessing. | First quarter: establish baselines. Second quarter: set targets. |
| **Metric without action** | Tracking something you will never act on is waste. | Ask: "If this metric changes, what would we do differently?" |

## Budget & Resource Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Premature scaling** | 70% of high-growth startups that failed did so from premature scaling. | Prove unit economics before scaling spend. |
| **Hiring a CMO too early** | A CMO needs a strategy to execute. At early stage, you need a doer. | First hire: T-shaped generalist who can execute. |
| **Outsourcing positioning** | No agency can articulate your positioning as well as you can. | Founder defines positioning. Agency executes tactics. |
| **Tool bloat** | Paying for 10 tools when you use 3 features from each. | Start with free tools. Upgrade only when you hit limits. |
| **No budget for testing** | 100% on proven = no innovation. | 70/20/10 split: proven / emerging / experimental. |

## Quality Tests

Run these at every gate before presenting to the user:

### The Competitor Test
Read every claim in the strategy. Could a competitor say the exact same thing? If yes, it is not differentiated enough.

### The Specificity Test
Check every sentence for vague language: "help businesses grow," "improve efficiency," "innovative solution." Replace with specific outcomes, numbers, and names.

### The Stranger Test
Show the one-liner and positioning to someone unfamiliar with the business. Can they explain what it does in 10 seconds?

### The "So What?" Test
After every claim, ask "so what?" If there is no compelling answer, the claim is noise.

### The Execution Test
For every recommendation: does the user have the time, money, and skills to actually do this? If not, either adjust the recommendation or flag the resource gap.

### The Kill Criteria Test
Every channel and tactic should have pre-defined failure criteria. "If [metric] does not reach [threshold] by [date], we stop."

### The Single-Page Test
Can the entire strategy fit on one page? If it cannot be summarized clearly in a page, it is too complex to execute.

## AI-Era Marketing Tells (2025-2026)

Content and strategy that screams "AI-generated":

| Tell | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic "comprehensive guide" framing** | Everyone is publishing AI-generated comprehensive guides. Zero differentiation. | Share a specific opinion or experience. |
| **Perfect grammar, zero personality** | Competent but indistinguishable from 1,000 other pieces. | Inject personal stories, humor, imperfect honesty. |
| **Listing benefits without proof** | "Save time and money" without evidence. | Attach a specific number, customer name, or case study. |
| **Strategy without trade-offs** | "Do everything, perfectly, for everyone." Real strategy involves saying no. | Name what you are NOT doing and why. |
| **Consensus opinions only** | "Content marketing is important." No kidding. | Take a contrarian position backed by evidence. |
| **No first-hand experience** | Writing about something you have never done. | Ground every recommendation in real results or honest uncertainty. |
