# Business Maturity — Reference Guide

## Purpose

Marketing strategy must match business maturity. Pre-PMF marketing is validation; post-PMF is repeatability; growth-stage is scalability. Applying the wrong approach at the wrong stage wastes resources and can actively harm the business. This reference covers how strategy adapts at each stage, founder-led marketing, and when to hire.

## The Four Maturity Stages

### Pre-PMF (Pre-Product-Market Fit)

**Signal:** No paying customers, or early customers who have not validated repeatable demand.

**Core principle:** Before product-market fit, all marketing activities are research activities. The goal is validation, not growth.

**What to do:**
- Customer development interviews (10-15 per persona minimum)
- Waitlist building with position-based incentives
- Community building around the problem (not the solution)
- Building in public (transparent development updates on LinkedIn/X)
- Feature validation with early users
- Behavioral segmentation of signups

**What NOT to do:**
- Hire a marketer or agency
- Run paid ads (accelerates churn without PMF)
- Build elaborate marketing infrastructure
- Optimize for growth before validating retention

**Budget:** Primarily time-based. 30-60% of any early revenue. Tool spend: $0-100/month.

**Time allocation (10 hrs/week):**
- 4 hours: Customer conversations and research
- 3 hours: Content creation (building in public)
- 2 hours: Community engagement
- 1 hour: Analytics and learning

**PMF validation signals:**
- 40%+ of users say they would be "very disappointed" without the product (Sean Ellis test)
- Organic growth without paid acquisition
- Low churn rate
- Customers refer without incentives
- Pull from market (demand exceeds supply)

### Post-PMF / Early Revenue

**Signal:** <$1M ARR/revenue. Founder doing most or all marketing. Some paying customers who retain.

**Core principle:** Transition from founder-as-marketer to founder-as-brand-voice with emerging systems.

**What to do:**
- Define positioning formally (Dunford framework)
- Build ICP from actual customer data
- Establish one primary channel and go deep
- Create messaging framework
- Set up minimum viable analytics (GA4 + UTMs + self-reported attribution)
- Build email capture and basic nurture sequence
- Start formal content creation (1 quality piece/week)

**What NOT to do:**
- Spread across 5 channels at once
- Hire a CMO (hire a T-shaped generalist instead)
- Run expensive paid campaigns before organic proves the funnel
- Stop founder-led marketing

**Budget:** 10-20% of revenue. Seed companies: $50K-$250K/year.

**Time allocation (15-20 hrs/week for founder):**
- 5-6 hours: Content creation for primary channel
- 3-4 hours: Community engagement and relationship building
- 3-4 hours: Email marketing and nurture
- 2-3 hours: Analytics, strategy, and planning
- 2-3 hours: Customer conversations (ongoing)

### Growth Stage

**Signal:** $1M-$10M revenue. Hiring first marketers. Repeatable sales process exists.

**Core principle:** Build scalable marketing systems that work without the founder doing everything.

**What to do:**
- Hire first marketing generalist (see hiring section below)
- Add a second channel based on data from primary
- Formalize content calendar and SEO strategy
- Implement proper CRM and marketing automation
- Test paid acquisition with $1,000-5,000/month
- Build case studies and social proof assets
- Develop partnership and referral programs
- Create marketing playbooks so it is not all in the founder's head

**What NOT to do:**
- Scale prematurely (70% of high-growth startups that failed did so from premature scaling)
- Hire specialists before generalists
- Stop founder involvement entirely
- Add channels without proving existing ones

**Budget:** 15-25% of revenue. Series A: $500K-$2M/year marketing allocation.

### Scaling

**Signal:** $10M+ revenue. Established marketing team. Multiple proven channels.

**Core principle:** Optimize efficiency, expand channels, and build a marketing organization.

**What to do:**
- Build full marketing team (see structure below)
- Implement multi-touch attribution
- Scale proven channels while maintaining efficiency
- Launch ABM (Account-Based Marketing) for enterprise
- Invest in brand marketing alongside performance
- Build marketing operations and data infrastructure
- Formal competitive intelligence program

**Budget:** 5-15% of revenue. Typically 30-50% of total S&M spend.

## Founder-Led Marketing

### Why Founders Should Do Marketing First

- No one else can articulate the origin story and vision authentically
- 70% of the buying journey happens before a prospect talks to sales — founders shape that journey
- Cost-effective: reduces reliance on expensive ads
- Compounds over time, creating audiences that drive sales without paid acquisition
- Melanie Perkins: used founder-led marketing to build Canva to $26B
- Jason Fried (Basecamp): built a multi-million dollar business primarily through opinionated content and books

### How to Do It in Limited Time

**The 90-10 Value Rule:** 90% value content, 10% promotion. Constant product pitching causes audience fatigue.

**Content pillars framework:** Develop 3-4 core themes aligning with business and personal expertise:
- Industry insights and opinions
- Behind-the-scenes building
- Customer stories and results
- Thought leadership and frameworks

**Platform focus:**
- **LinkedIn:** Primary for B2B. Employee accounts drive 30% of a company's post engagement. Employee networks are 10x larger than company followers.
- **Twitter/X:** Rewards concise, insight-heavy content. Strong for tech and investors.
- **TikTok:** Better for consumer-facing. 2.50% engagement rate vs Instagram 0.50%.

**Weekly planning system:**
1. Batch content creation (not daily posting)
2. 3-4 content pillars
3. Apply 90-10 value rule
4. Schedule strategically
5. Plan quarterly themes with weekly execution
6. Track performance weekly

### Scaling Without Abandoning

As the company grows, build systems that amplify founder-led approaches while maintaining authenticity:
- Content teams support research and formatting
- Founder provides voice, opinions, and story hooks
- Team repurposes founder content across channels
- Founder shifts from daily posting to weekly thought leadership

## When and Who to Hire

### First Marketing Hire

**Timing:** After initial PMF and some revenue (post-Seed or early Series A). If marketing centers on content/community, can be earlier.

**Profile options:**

| Approach | Who | When |
|----------|-----|------|
| **SignalFire recommendation** | Product Marketer (PMM) with 5+ years | When positioning and messaging are critical |
| **Kracov recommendation** | Junior generalist, first or second job | When PMF is unproven, need experimentation |
| **First Round recommendation** | Match to GTM motion | PLG = growth marketer. Top-down = product marketer |

**Key characteristics:**
- T-shaped generalist (broad knowledge, deep in one skill)
- Can execute, not just strategize ("button clicker" not just theorist)
- Strong writer
- Growth mindset, coachable
- Ruthless prioritizer
- Prepared for IC-level work for several quarters

**De-risk the hire:** Request a take-home marketing plan. Consider contract-to-hire.

### Team Structure by Size

**1 person (founder or first hire):**
- 40% content creation (primary channel)
- 20% website + conversion optimization
- 20% strategy + analytics
- 10% email
- 10% distribution

**5 people:**
| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Head of Marketing | Strategy, planning, analytics |
| Content Marketer | Blog, SEO content, social |
| Demand Gen / Growth | Paid, email, conversion |
| Designer | Brand, web, content design |
| Product Marketer or SDR | Messaging, enablement, or outbound |

**10 people:**
| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| VP Marketing | Strategy, budget, leadership |
| Product Marketing Manager | Positioning, launches, competitive |
| Content Lead + Writer(s) | Strategy + production |
| SEO Specialist | Technical SEO, keyword strategy |
| Demand Gen Manager | Paid, email, funnel |
| Social Media Manager | Community, social channels |
| Designer(s) | Brand, web, campaign creative |
| Marketing Ops / Analytics | Attribution, tooling, reporting |

### Hire vs Agency vs DIY

| Function | DIY/Founder | Freelancer | Agency | In-House |
|----------|------------|------------|--------|----------|
| Brand voice & positioning | Best | — | — | After PMF |
| Content strategy | Best early | Good for execution | — | First hire territory |
| Content writing | OK initially | Best value at scale | Good | Second hire |
| SEO | Learn basics | Technical audit | Ongoing | After $1M+ |
| Paid acquisition | Small testing | — | Best (expertise) | After proven |
| Social media | Founder-led | Good for execution | OK | Junior hire |
| Email marketing | DIY with tools | — | Not needed | Part of generalist |
| PR/media | Founder outreach | — | Specialized firms | Rarely in-house early |

**Cost comparison:**
- Two mid-level hires: $324K-$364K/year fully loaded
- Full agency: $300K-$540K/year ($25K-$45K/month)
- Sustainable in-house when marketing spend is <10% of revenue

## Signs the Strategy Needs to Change

### PMF Warning Signs
- High churn rate
- Growth stalls
- Heavy reliance on paid to sustain any growth
- Customers not referring

### Channel Exhaustion Signals
- Decreasing CTR across campaigns
- Rising CAC exceeding 2-3x previous levels
- CAC payback exceeding 12 months
- Little organic traffic from primary channels

### Decision Framework
- CAC exceeds 2x average: investigate within 7 days
- CAC exceeds 3x average: pause and audit the channel
- CAC payback exceeds 12 months: reduce allocation by 50%

### Review Cadence
- Monthly: review MER and channel CAC
- Quarterly: deep-dive optimization
- Annually: full strategy overhaul
- Dynamic: adjust when unit economics break
