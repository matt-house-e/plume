# Page Templates — Reference Guide

## Purpose

Section-by-section copy frameworks for every common page type. Each template shows the structure, the job of each section, approximate word counts, and example copy. Use these as starting scaffolds — adapt to the specific project, never fill in mechanically.

## Homepage

The homepage is the most important page. It has 3-5 seconds to communicate: what you do, who it's for, and why it matters.

### Section Structure

| Section | Job | Word Count | Must Include |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hero** | Hook + value prop + CTA | 30-60 words | H1 headline, subheadline, primary CTA, optional secondary CTA |
| **Social proof bar** | Instant credibility | 5-15 words + logos | Client logos or "Trusted by X" or review score |
| **Problem / pain** | Show you understand the audience | 40-80 words | Name the specific pain they feel |
| **Solution overview** | Introduce your approach | 60-120 words | 3-4 key benefits with brief explanations |
| **How it works** | Reduce complexity | 60-100 words | 3-step process or visual workflow |
| **Featured work / results** | Prove it works | 40-80 words per item | 2-3 case study snippets with metrics |
| **Testimonials** | Third-party validation | 30-60 words per quote | 2-3 testimonials with full attribution |
| **Final CTA** | Close the page with action | 20-40 words | Restate value prop, repeat primary CTA |

### Hero Copy Formula

```
H1: [Outcome the audience wants] — [differentiator or specificity]
Subheadline: [How you deliver it, in one sentence, for whom]
Primary CTA: [Action verb] + [what they get]
Secondary CTA: [Lower-commitment alternative]
```

**Example (SaaS):**
> **H1:** Ship products your customers actually want.
> **Sub:** User research tools that give you real feedback in hours, not weeks — so you can build with confidence.
> **CTA:** Start Free Trial
> **Secondary:** See How It Works

**Example (Agency):**
> **H1:** Websites that turn visitors into customers.
> **Sub:** Conversion-focused design and copy for B2B companies tired of pretty sites that don't perform.
> **CTA:** See Our Work
> **Secondary:** Book a Strategy Call

### Homepage Anti-Patterns

- Hero headline that describes the company, not the customer's outcome ("We are a leading provider of..." → no)
- No CTA above the fold
- Social proof buried at the bottom
- Feature list instead of benefit-led sections
- More than one competing CTA in the hero

## About Page

The about page is often the second most-visited page. Its job is to build trust and connection, not just list credentials.

### Section Structure

| Section | Job | Word Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening statement** | Why you exist (not what you do — the homepage covered that) | 40-80 words |
| **Origin story** | How and why the company/person started | 100-200 words |
| **Mission / values** | What you stand for | 60-120 words |
| **Team** | Humanize the brand | 40-80 words per bio |
| **Credentials** | Proof of expertise | 40-80 words |
| **CTA** | Convert interest to action | 20-40 words |

### Story Framework for About Pages

Use the **Founder's Journey** structure:
1. **The frustration** — "We kept seeing [problem] and it drove us crazy."
2. **The insight** — "We realized [insight that others missed]."
3. **The decision** — "So we built [solution]."
4. **The proof** — "Since then, [specific accomplishments]."
5. **The invitation** — "If that resonates, [CTA]."

### Team Bio Formula

```
[Name] — [Role]
[One sentence about what they do at the company]
[One sentence about their background/expertise — specific, not generic]
[One sentence that humanizes them — interest, quirk, fun fact]
```

**Example:**
> **Sarah Chen — Head of Design**
> Sarah leads the design team and obsesses over the details you'll never consciously notice but always feel. Before joining, she spent 6 years at Figma designing the collaboration features used by millions of teams. She collects vintage synthesizers and believes every interface should feel like a musical instrument.

## Services Page

### Individual Service Structure

| Section | Job | Word Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Headline** | Benefit-led description of the service | 5-12 words |
| **Overview** | What the service is and who it's for | 60-100 words |
| **What's included** | Specific deliverables | Bullet list, 4-8 items |
| **Process** | How the engagement works | 3-5 steps, 20-40 words each |
| **Results** | Proof it works | 1-2 case study snippets or metrics |
| **Pricing** (optional) | Remove price uncertainty | Starting price or "from $X" |
| **CTA** | Next step | Button + 1-line supporting text |

**Headline formula:** "[Outcome] [Service Type]" — lead with the benefit, not the deliverable.
- "Websites That Convert" not "Web Design Services"
- "Copy That Sells" not "Copywriting Services"
- "Brands People Remember" not "Brand Identity Design"

## Product Page

### Section Structure (E-commerce / SaaS)

| Section | Job | Word Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Product title** | Clear, descriptive, keyword-rich | 3-10 words |
| **Short description** | What it is and who it's for in one sentence | 15-30 words |
| **Key benefits** | Top 3-4 reasons to buy, benefit-led | 3-4 bullets, 8-15 words each |
| **Full description** | Detailed explanation with sensory/specific language | 100-200 words |
| **Specifications/details** | Technical details, sizing, materials, compatibility | Structured list |
| **Social proof** | Reviews, ratings, "X people bought this" | Variable |
| **Cross-sell** | Related products or upsell | 1-2 sentences + product links |
| **CTA** | Clear purchase action | Button + supporting text |

### Product Description Formula

```
[What it is] + [who it's for] + [key benefit].

[2-3 sentences expanding on the experience or outcome — use sensory
language for physical products, outcome language for digital].

[Feature → benefit → proof pattern, repeated 2-3 times]:
- [Feature]: [Benefit it provides]. [Proof point or specific detail].

[Social proof line: "Rated X/5 by Y customers" or "Used by Z teams"]
```

**Example (physical product):**
> **Merino Wool Travel Blanket**
> The only blanket that packs smaller than a water bottle and keeps you warm at 30,000 feet.
>
> Woven from 18.5-micron Australian merino — soft enough to sleep on, tough enough for weekly washing. Folds into its own built-in pouch (4" x 6") and clips to any bag.
>
> - **Temperature regulating:** Merino naturally adapts to your body heat. No more choosing between too-hot and too-cold.
> - **Odor resistant:** Wear it 10 flights before it needs a wash. Merino's lanolin does the work.
> - **Machine washable:** Cold cycle, tumble dry low. No special care required.
>
> 4.8/5 from 2,300+ travelers. "The only travel blanket I've ever kept." — Sarah K.

**Example (SaaS product):**
> **Smart Analytics Dashboard**
> See what's working in your marketing — without a data science degree.
>
> Connects to your existing tools in 2 minutes. Pulls data from Google Analytics, your CRM, and ad platforms into one view. Surfaces the insights that matter — which campaigns drive revenue, which pages lose visitors, where to invest next.
>
> - **One-click reports:** Generate board-ready reports in seconds, not hours.
> - **Anomaly detection:** Get alerted when a metric spikes or drops before it becomes a problem.
> - **Plain-English insights:** "Your blog traffic grew 34% this month, driven by 3 posts about pricing strategy."

### Feature-to-Benefit Translation Table

Use this pattern for every product feature:

| Feature (What It Has) | Benefit (What It Means for Them) | Proof (Why They Should Believe It) |
|---|---|---|
| 256-bit encryption | Customer data stays private — even from us | SOC 2 Type II certified |
| 99.99% uptime SLA | The tool is there when customers need it | $10K credit if we miss it |
| 14-day free trial | Try everything before committing a dollar | No credit card required |

## Pricing Page

### Structure

| Section | Job |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Reframe pricing as choosing a growth path, not spending money |
| **Plan cards** (2-4) | Name, price, one-line descriptor, feature list, CTA per plan |
| **Feature comparison table** | Detailed comparison for analytical buyers |
| **FAQ** | Handle pricing objections (5-8 questions) |
| **Guarantee** | Risk reversal |
| **Enterprise/custom CTA** | Catch prospects who don't fit standard plans |

### Plan Card Copy

```
[Plan Name]
[One-line descriptor: who this is for]
$[Price]/[period]
[5-8 features, most differentiating first]
[CTA button]
```

**Plan naming principles:**
- Avoid: Basic, Standard, Premium (generic, "Basic" has negative connotations)
- Better: Starter, Growth, Scale (aspirational, stage-based)
- Best: Audience-based names that signal fit (Solo, Team, Business)

### Pricing FAQ Questions to Answer

1. Can I change plans later?
2. Is there a free trial / money-back guarantee?
3. What happens if I outgrow my plan?
4. Do you offer discounts for annual billing / nonprofits / startups?
5. What's not included?
6. How does billing work?
7. Can I cancel anytime?

## Contact Page

### Structure

| Section | Job | Copy Length |
|---|---|---|
| **Headline** | Invite conversation, not just collect info | 5-12 words |
| **Introduction** | Set expectations and reduce friction | 2-3 sentences |
| **Form** | Capture the inquiry | Labels + helper text |
| **Alternative methods** | Phone, email, social for those who prefer them | List |
| **Response time** | Set expectations | One sentence |

**Headline examples:**
- "Let's Talk About Your Project" (agency)
- "Get in Touch" (simple, universal)
- "Start a Conversation" (approachable)
- NOT: "Contact Us" (cold, corporate)

**Introduction formula:**
"[What happens after they submit]. [How long until you respond]. [What to include for the fastest reply]."

**Example:** "Tell us about your project and we'll get back to you within one business day. The more detail you share, the more useful our first conversation will be."

## Landing Page

### Long-Form Structure (for cold traffic / high-ticket)

| Section | Word Count | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | 30-60 | Hook + promise + CTA |
| Problem | 80-150 | Agitate the pain |
| Solution | 60-100 | Introduce the approach |
| Benefits | 100-200 | 3-6 benefits with detail |
| Social proof | 60-120 | Testimonials + metrics |
| How it works | 60-100 | 3-5 steps |
| Objection handling | 100-200 | FAQ or inline counter-arguments |
| Pricing / offer | 60-100 | What they get + what it costs |
| Guarantee | 30-60 | Risk reversal |
| Final CTA | 20-40 | Restate value + CTA |

### Short-Form Structure (for warm traffic / low-commitment)

Hero → 3 Benefits → Social Proof → CTA. Total: 200-400 words.

## FAQ Page

### Question Writing Rules

- Write questions in the customer's voice, not the company's voice
- Use "I" or "my" in questions: "How long will my project take?" not "What is the typical project timeline?"
- Start with the most common objection, not the most basic question
- Each answer: 2-4 sentences. Link to longer content if needed.
- End 2-3 answers with a soft CTA: "Still have questions? [Contact us]."

## 404 Page

### Copy Elements

1. **Headline:** Acknowledge the error with personality — "This page took a wrong turn."
2. **Body:** Brief explanation + helpful next steps (2-3 sentences max)
3. **Navigation:** Link to homepage, popular pages, search bar
4. **Tone:** Match brand voice. The 404 page is a chance to show personality in an unexpected place.

**Example:**
> **H1:** Well, this is awkward.
> **Body:** The page you're looking for has moved, been deleted, or never existed in the first place. Here are some places that definitely do exist:
> [Homepage] [Our Work] [Contact Us]

## Thank-You / Confirmation Page

### Must-Have Copy Elements

1. **Confirmation headline:** "You're in!" / "Message sent!" / "Thanks, [Name]!"
2. **What happens next:** "We'll review your message and reply within 24 hours."
3. **While you wait:** Link to helpful content (blog, case studies, social accounts)
4. **Secondary CTA:** A lower-commitment next action (follow on social, read a post)

Never waste the thank-you page. The visitor just demonstrated high intent — give them something valuable.

## Portfolio Project Page

### Section Structure

| Section | Job | Word Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Project title** | Name the work | 3-8 words |
| **Overview / challenge** | What the client needed and why | 60-100 words |
| **Approach** | What was done and how | 80-150 words |
| **Outcome** | Results, impact, metrics if available | 60-100 words |
| **Visuals** | Screenshots, photos, or deliverable samples | Alt text for each |
| **Testimonial** (optional) | Client quote about the experience | 20-40 words |
| **Next project link** | Keep them browsing | Link + project name |
| **CTA** | Convert project interest into inquiry | Button + 1-line text |

### Project Description Formula (for portfolio grid thumbnails)

```
[Project Name] — [Client/Industry]
[One sentence: what was done + key outcome]
```

**Example:** "Meridian Rebrand — Fintech / Brand identity and website that helped close a $4M Series A."

## Testimonials Page (Local Business / Services)

### Structure

| Section | Job |
|---|---|
| **Headline** | Frame the testimonials: "What Our Clients Say" or "Don't Take Our Word for It" |
| **Featured testimonial** | Longest, most detailed review — with full attribution and photo if possible |
| **Grid of testimonials** | 6-12 reviews, each with: quote, name, role/company (or "Verified Customer"), star rating if applicable |
| **CTA** | Convert trust into action: "Ready to join them?" + primary CTA |

### Testimonial Editing Rules

- Keep the customer's voice — don't polish away the personality
- Edit for brevity: cut throat-clearing ("I just wanted to say that..."), keep the meat
- The strongest testimonials are specific: "Revenue up 34%" > "Great service!"
- Always include attribution: full name + role/company. Anonymous testimonials are 50% less persuasive.
- Get explicit permission before publishing
