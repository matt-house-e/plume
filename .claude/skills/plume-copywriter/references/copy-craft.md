# Copy Craft — Reference Guide

## Purpose

This is the core technique file — headline formulas, body copy patterns, storytelling frameworks, power words, and rhythm. These are the tools a copywriter reaches for when facing a blank page. Every formula includes an example because a formula without an example is just theory.

## Headline Formulas

### The 12 Proven Headline Patterns

Headlines get 5x more readership than body copy (David Ogilvy). Headlines with numbers outperform those without — odd numbers slightly outperform even (BuzzSumo analysis, 100M+ headlines). Question headlines get ~14% higher CTR in search results (Backlinko, 2024). However, "power words" in titles can actually decrease search CTR by ~14% — Google users now prefer straightforward, specific titles over hype (Backlinko, 2024). The trend is toward clarity and specificity over cleverness.

| # | Formula | Example | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **How to [Achieve X] Without [Pain Y]** | "How to Double Your Email List Without Buying Ads" | How-to content, service pages |
| 2 | **[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]** | "7 Ways to Cut Your AWS Bill by 40%" | Blog posts, listicles |
| 3 | **The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]** | "The No-BS Guide to Landing Page Copy" | Long-form content, pillar pages |
| 4 | **Why [Surprising Thing] [Unexpected Outcome]** | "Why Your Best Customers Never Read Your Blog" | Thought leadership, contrarian takes |
| 5 | **[Do This], Not [That]** | "Write Headlines, Not Clickbait" | Comparison, opinion |
| 6 | **[Specific Result] in [Specific Timeframe]** | "1,000 New Subscribers in 30 Days" | Case studies, landing pages |
| 7 | **What [Expert/Group] [Does] That [You Don't]** | "What Top-Converting SaaS Sites Do Differently" | Authority content |
| 8 | **Stop [Doing Painful Thing]** | "Stop Writing Homepage Copy That Sounds Like Everyone Else" | Pain-point openers |
| 9 | **[Audience], Here's [What You Need]** | "Freelancers, Here's How to Charge What You're Worth" | Audience-targeted pages |
| 10 | **The [Unexpected] Way to [Achieve X]** | "The Laziest Way to Generate B2B Leads" | Curiosity-driven content |
| 11 | **[Product/Service] for [Specific Audience]** | "Accounting Software for Freelancers Who Hate Math" | Product pages, niche landing pages |
| 12 | **[Outcome] — Without [Expected Cost]** | "Enterprise Security — Without the Enterprise Price Tag" | Value-prop headlines |

### Headline Quality Tests

- **The "who cares?" test:** Read the headline as a busy stranger. Would you stop scrolling?
- **The specificity test:** Does it contain a number, a name, or a timeframe? Specifics outperform generics.
- **The competitor test:** Could your competitor use this exact headline? If yes → not specific enough.
- **The clarity test:** Does a reader know what they'll get? Cleverness that sacrifices clarity fails.

## Storytelling Frameworks

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

The most versatile copywriting framework. Works for nearly any awareness level.

| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | Name the pain the reader recognizes | "Your website gets traffic but nobody converts." |
| **Agitate** | Make the pain vivid and urgent | "Every day, hundreds of visitors land on your site, skim your homepage, and leave — taking their wallets with them. That traffic cost you money. And it's evaporating." |
| **Solution** | Present the answer | "Our conversion copywriting service turns browsers into buyers. Average client sees 2.4x more leads within 60 days." |

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

Best for cold audiences who don't yet know they have a problem.

| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll | "91% of B2B websites fail the 5-second test." |
| **Interest** | Give them a reason to keep reading | "We tested 200 B2B homepages. Only 18 could communicate their value proposition in under 5 seconds." |
| **Desire** | Make them want the outcome | "Companies that pass the 5-second test convert visitors at 3x the industry average." |
| **Action** | Tell them exactly what to do | "Get your free 5-second audit. We'll record 10 testers and show you exactly where visitors get lost." |

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Best for aspirational messaging and case studies.

| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Before** | Paint the current painful reality | "You're spending $5K/month on ads that send traffic to a homepage written in 2019." |
| **After** | Paint the desired future | "Imagine every ad click landing on copy that speaks directly to your customer's biggest frustration — and offers a clear path to solving it." |
| **Bridge** | Show how to get from Before to After | "That's exactly what we did for Acme Co. New homepage copy → 47% increase in demo requests in 6 weeks." |

### PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Transformation, Offer, Response

Extended framework for long-form sales copy and landing pages.

| Stage | Job |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Identify the pain point |
| **Amplify** | Show the cost of not solving it |
| **Story** | Tell a relatable story of someone with the same problem |
| **Transformation** | Show the before/after |
| **Offer** | Present what you're offering |
| **Response** | Ask for the specific action |

### 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

Best for service pages and product descriptions.

| Stage | Job | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Promise** | Lead with the benefit | "Launch your SaaS in half the time." |
| **Picture** | Help them visualize success | "While competitors are still in development, you're already onboarding users." |
| **Proof** | Back it up | "We've launched 40+ SaaS products. Average time to market: 12 weeks." |
| **Push** | Create urgency to act now | "We take on 3 new clients per quarter. Q2 has one spot left." |

## Body Copy Patterns

### The Inverted Pyramid

Lead with the most important information. Each paragraph can be the last one read and still deliver value. Structure: conclusion → supporting details → background.

### The 1-2-3-1 Pattern

For feature/benefit sections:
1. **One** clear benefit headline
2. **Two-three** sentences of supporting copy
3. **Three** specific proof points (bullet list)
4. **One** CTA

### Bucket Brigades

Short transitional phrases that pull readers into the next paragraph. Place them at natural drop-off points:

| Bucket Brigade | Use When |
|---|---|
| Here's the thing: | Introducing a key insight |
| But here's the catch: | Introducing a complication |
| It gets better: | Building excitement |
| Think about it: | Encouraging reflection |
| Here's what that means for you: | Translating feature to benefit |
| The result? | Introducing an outcome |
| Bottom line: | Summarizing |
| And the best part? | Highlighting an unexpected benefit |

### The Feature-to-Benefit Bridge

Never state a feature without bridging to the benefit. Use "so you can" or "which means" as the bridge:

- **Feature only:** "256-bit encryption" ← So what?
- **Feature + bridge + benefit:** "256-bit encryption, so your customer data stays private — even from us."

## Power Words by Category

### Urgency
Now, today, hurry, limited, deadline, last chance, before, running out, while, instant, immediately, fast

### Exclusivity
Exclusive, members-only, insider, invitation, VIP, private, first access, behind the scenes, handpicked, curated

### Trust
Guaranteed, proven, certified, verified, backed, tested, secure, protected, authentic, transparent, no-risk

### Curiosity
Secret, hidden, little-known, surprising, unexpected, revealed, truth about, what nobody tells you, behind, the real reason

### Safety
Safe, risk-free, money-back, cancel anytime, no obligation, no commitment, try free, protected, worry-free

### Value
Free, bonus, save, discount, extra, included, complimentary, at no cost, unlimited, premium

**Usage rule:** Power words are seasoning, not the meal. One per headline maximum. Two per paragraph maximum. Overuse creates hype copy that undermines trust.

## Rhythm and Readability

### Sentence Length Rules

- **Vary sentence length.** This is the most important rhythm rule. Mix short punches (3-8 words) with medium explanations (12-20 words). An occasional longer sentence (25+ words) works if followed by a short one.
- **The Gary Provost rhythm test:**
  > "This sentence has five words. Here are five more words. Five-word sentences are fine. But several together become monotonous. Listen to what is happening. The writing is getting boring. The sound of it drones. It's like a stuck record. The ear demands some variety. Now listen. I vary the sentence length, and I create music. Music. The writing sings."

### Paragraph Length for Web

- **1-3 sentences per paragraph.** Maximum 4 lines displayed on screen.
- **One idea per paragraph.** If a paragraph covers two ideas, split it.
- **Subheadings every 200-300 words.** Subheadings should tell a story on their own — a reader who only reads subheadings should get the gist.

### Readability Targets

| Context | Flesch-Kincaid Grade | Flesch Reading Ease |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer web copy | Grade 6-8 | 60-80 |
| B2B / professional | Grade 8-10 | 50-65 |
| Technical / developer | Grade 9-12 | 40-55 |
| Legal / compliance | Grade 12+ | 30-45 |

Target the lower end of each range. Simpler is almost always better. Hemingway Editor (hemingwayapp.com) is the fastest way to check.

## Opening Hooks

### 7 First-Line Techniques

| Technique | Example | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Startling statistic** | "68% of online shopping carts are abandoned before checkout." | Data-driven audiences, blog posts |
| **Provocative question** | "What if everything you know about SEO is five years out of date?" | Thought leadership |
| **Direct address** | "You've tried three agencies. None of them moved the needle." | Landing pages, pain-aware audiences |
| **Contradiction** | "The best-performing landing pages have almost no copy." | Contrarian content |
| **Micro-story** | "Last Tuesday, our client Sarah opened her dashboard and saw something she'd never seen: a full pipeline." | Case studies, testimonial-led pages |
| **Bold claim** | "We'll double your conversion rate in 90 days or you pay nothing." | High-confidence offers with guarantees |
| **Empathy statement** | "Writing your own website copy at midnight — again — isn't what you signed up for." | Service pages, audience-specific pain |
